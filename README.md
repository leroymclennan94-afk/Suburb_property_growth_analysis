The output of this project is a choropleth map of the state of Victoria, with suburbs coloured according to their investment returns. This was done to provide a visual of the different areas within the state where property investment may be a good or bad idea. The map can be toggled to differentiate between house and unit investment returns.

To facilitate fair comparisons, as a) some suburbs you may be able to purchase a property and have leftover savings, and b) some suburbs you can buy with a mortgage but will have remaining disposable income after accounting for mortgae payments, any money that is remaining after accounting for housing costs is invested in a generic index based ETF. The return characteristics of this ETF can be modified, and are currently based on the long term data for the S&P500.

The buying strategy assumes that the buyer puts all of their savings into the property at the time of purchase, to minimise the mortgage repayments, and then just makes the required mortgage repayments after this. An alternative strategy in future projects could be to put the minimum deposit on the property whilst still satisfying loan servicability, and put the remaining funds into an ETF.
